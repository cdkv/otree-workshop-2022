In this stage of the experiment you will be asked to revise your previous decisions in case there are inconsistencies between your commitment to a rule in the first stage and your decisions in the second stage.

In particular, we will explicitly present to you the rule and your chosen commitment, as well as the decision that is inconsistent with your rule commitment. This way, you can analyze the rule and your choices together.

You will then have the opportunity to make changes if you wish to do so. (1) You might decide that you want to change your distribution choice to align with what the rule would have chosen for you. (2) Or you might decide that the rule should not always be applied, so you can withdraw from the rule and uncommit. (3) Note that you can also stick to your decisions in the earlier stages and not make any changes, i.e. you can leave both your commitment to the rule and your distribution choice as they were.

Again, your decisions are consequential. Specifically, we will implement an income distribution for a group of 10 randomly drawn individuals based on your choices.

This is how it works:

At the end of the experiment, a random mechanism will determine which of your decisions is chosen for implementation. Each decision in this stage can be chosen for implementation. You should always choose the option that you actually prefer, because this is the only way to increase the chance that your preferred option is implemented.

Similar to stage one, there is some pair of income distributions associated with each rule (not necessarily the same pair which you are given an option to revise). The two distributions differ from each other such that the rule can be applied in the way it was described to you. This means that it is possible to infer which distribution should be implemented based on the rule.

If the rule choice is picked by the random mechanism to be relevant and you are commited to the rule, then the choice in that pair of distributions (not necessarily the same pair which you are given an option to revise) is made automatically based on the rule, and this distribution will be implemented.

If the rule choice is picked by the random mechanism to be relevant and you are not commited to the rule, then the pair will be presented to you to make a decision manually, and the distribution you choose will be implemented.

If the distribution choice is picked by the random mechanism to be relevant, then the payoffs are determined by the distribution choice you make. If you choose program A in this pair, then program B from this pair will be implemented. If you choose program B in this pair, then program B from this pair will be implemented.
