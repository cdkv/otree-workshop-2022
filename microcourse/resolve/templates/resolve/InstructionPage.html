In this stage of the experiment you will be asked to revise your previous decisions in case there are inconsistencies between your commitment to a principle in the first stage and your decisions in the second stage.

In particular, we will explicitly present to you the principle and your chosen commitment, as well as the decision that is inconsistent with your principle commitment. This way, you can analyze the principle and your choices together.

You will then have the opportunity to make changes if you wish to do so. You might decide that you want to change your distribution choice to align with what the principle would have chosen for you. Or you might decide that the principle should not always be applied, so you can "un-commit" to the principle. Note that you can also stick to your decisions in the earlier stages and not make any changes, i.e. you can leave both your commitment to the principle and your distribution choice as they were.

Again, your decisions are consequential. Specifically, we will implement an income distribution for a group of 10 randomly drawn individuals based on your choices.

This is how it works:

At the end of the experiment, a random mechanism will determine which of your decisions is chosen for implementation. Each of the choices that will be presented to you during the revision stage have an equal chance to be chosen by the random mechanism.

Similar to stage one, there is a pair of income distributions associated with each principle. The two distributions differ from each other such that the principle can be applied in the way it was described to you. This means that it is possible to infer which distribution should be implemented based on the principle.

If the principle is chosen by the random mechanism to be relevant and you commited to the principle, then the choice between the two distributions is made automatically based on the principle, and this distribution will be implemented.

If the principle is chosen by the random mechanism to be relevant and you chose not to commit, then the pair will be presented to you to make a decision manually, and the distribution you choose will be implemented.

If the distribution choice is chosen by the random mechanism to be relevant and you chose program A in this pair, then program A from this pair will be implemented.

If the distribution choice is chosen by the random mechanism to be relevant and you chose program B in this pair, then program B from this pair will be implemented.
